---

# 1. Setup

- the system state \( |\Psi_S\rangle \)
- the receiver's POVM element \( E_i \)

The raw probability is:

\[
p(i) = \langle \Psi_S | E_i | \Psi_S \rangle.
\]

USP treats both the system and receiver as **Gaussian-shaped** probability amplitude profiles in the relevant subspace.

\[
g_i(x) = \frac{1}{(2\pi \sigma_R^2)^{1/4}}

- \(\sigma_S\) = system spread

\[

\sqrt{\frac{2\sigma_S\sigma_R}{\sigma_S^2 + \sigma_R^2}}

This is the central result from which the detuning law emerges.
